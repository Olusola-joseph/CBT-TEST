## Passage 1
The Industrial Revolution began in Britain in the late 18th century and spread to other parts of Europe and North America during the 19th century. It was characterized by the shift from manual labor to machine-based manufacturing processes. Key innovations included the steam engine, textile machinery, and improvements in iron production. The revolution dramatically changed society, leading to urbanization, changes in social classes, and new forms of employment.

### Questions for Passage 1
1. Where did the Industrial Revolution begin?
2. What characterized the shift during the Industrial Revolution?
3. Name three key innovations mentioned in the passage.
4. How did the Industrial Revolution affect society?

## Passage 2
Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While climate variations occur naturally, scientific evidence shows that human activities since the 1800s have been the main driver of climate change. Burning fossil fuels releases greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, trapping heat and causing global warming. Effects include rising sea levels, extreme weather events, and changes to ecosystems.

### Questions for Passage 2
1. What is climate change?
2. What has been the main driver of climate change since the 1800s?
3. Name two greenhouse gases mentioned in the passage.
4. List three effects of climate change mentioned in the passage.

## Passage 3
Artificial intelligence (AI) is transforming numerous industries by enabling machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks include learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and language understanding. AI technologies such as machine learning, natural language processing, and computer vision are being integrated into healthcare, finance, transportation, and entertainment sectors. While AI offers many benefits, it also raises ethical concerns about privacy, job displacement, and decision-making transparency.
